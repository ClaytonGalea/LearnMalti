Research Title:

Design and Development of a Maltese Language Learning Game: A Comparative Study on Learner Engagement Between Gamified and Non-Gamified Modes


Main area of study being proposed: 

The proposed study focuses on the application of gamification techniques in e-learning systems, specifically in the context of minority language education. It combines principles from game design, educational technology, and human-computer interaction (HCI) to explore how interactive, reward-based mechanics can enhance engagement, motivation, and knowledge retention in Maltese language learning.
Technically, the project lies within the field of Web Development and Educational Software, utilizing .NET 8 (Blazor) for front-end development, ASP.NET Core for backend logic, and SQL Server for data management.

 
Personal Motivation for the Choice of Research Theme. 

My motivation for choosing this research theme comes from a deep interest in both technology and education, and a desire to contribute to the preservation and accessibility of the Maltese language. As a Maltese speaker and software developer, I’ve noticed that while there are some Maltese learning tools available, few of them explore how modern game design principles can make language learning more engaging and effective especially for children and foreigners who often find traditional methods challenging.

By combining my passion for software development, game mechanics, and language learning, I want to create a platform that not only teaches Maltese in a fun and interactive way but also provides valuable research insights on how gamification can enhance motivation and learning outcomes. This project allows me to bring together creativity, technical skill, and cultural appreciation into one meaningful study.

 
Hypotheses and/or Research Question/s 

Higher levels of motivation, engagement, and knowledge retention are anticipated from learners using the gamified Maltese learning platform compared to those using the non-gamified version.

•	When compared to the non-gamified version, do gamification features (timers, collectibles, badges, and audio feedback) increase learner motivation and engagement?

•	Does using gamified activities to learn Maltese grammar and vocabulary lead to better retention compared to non-gamified instruction?

•	What impressions do students have of gamification in minority language instruction?

Target Participants and Research Methods for Data Collection and Analysis 
This study will employ a mixed-methods approach to examine the impact of gamification on young students' Maltese language acquisition. Both quantitative and qualitative data will be gathered in order to provide a more complete picture of how gamified features impact learner motivation, engagement, and achievement by means of digital technology.


Participants and Sampling

About 40 students from two classes (Year 1 and Year 2) will take part. They will be selected through convenience sampling based on school approval and class availability. There will be two student groups created:
•	Gamified Group: Will make use of the platform’s gamified version which includes features such as XP and badges.
•	Control Group: Will make use of the platform's non-gamified version, which lacks game-based features but has the same structure and content.

All participants are under the age of sixteen, and a digital pop-up form within the application will be used to get parents' or guardians' informed consent. The goal of the study, data privacy, and withdrawal rights will all be spelt out in detail in this form.

Prototype Implementation

The digital platform will offer structured Maltese instruction covering basic vocabulary, grammar, and pronunciation through visual and auditory cues. The following mechanics will be present in the gamified prototype:
•	Points and Rewards: awarded for completing tasks correctly.
•	Badges and Unlockable Levels: to reflect progress and achievement.
•	Timers: to encourage time management and task focus.
•	Mini Games and Challenges: for vocabulary retention and revision.
•	XP and Level progression.
These features are intended to boost time-on-task, facilitate self-paced learning, and offer prompt, insightful feedback.
To provide a neutral baseline for comparison, the control version will retain the same educational content but eliminate all gamification features.

Data Collection

•	Quantitative Data: Pre- and post-assessments of grammar and vocabulary learning.
•	Qualitative Data: Open-ended questions (answered with teacher assistance) to collect student opinions regarding preference, enjoyment, and ease of use.

Data Analysis

Quantitative data will be extracted from a structured database and examined using paired t-tests and descriptive statistics to compare performance between the experimental and control groups. Real-time tracking and analysis will be possible thanks to the database's storage of participants' progress, test results, and activity metrics. Qualitative answers to open-ended questions will be thematically coded and compared between the two groups to spot patterns in learner attitudes and engagement. This combined strategy will assist in determining the relationship between various forms of platform interaction and learning objectives.


Anticipated Contributions of the Study. 

This study is expected to make both academic and practical contributions.

Academic Contributions:

•	It will provide observable evidence on how gamification impacts engagement, motivation, and knowledge retention in the context of minority language learning on the Maltese language.
•	The findings will advance our understanding of how e-learning design, educational gamification, and technology enhances language learning.
•	The study will provide perspectives on which of the gamified game mechanics such as level progression, badges or adaptive difficulty have the greatest effects on learning outcomes by contrasting gamified and non-gamified learning modes.

Practical Contributions:

•	The project will produce a functional gamified Maltese learning platform that can be used by educators, learners, and researchers for further development or classroom integration.
•	It will demonstrate how .NET 8 with the help of Blazor can be leveraged to create interactive, gamified learning environments with built-in data collection. 
•	The findings could help guide future Maltese language learning applications to be more engaging and effective, supporting both cultural preservation and language accessibility.
